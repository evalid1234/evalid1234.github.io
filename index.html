Data Structures & Algos

  • Summary: Find formal parameters( namely, space and time complexity) as well as methods of comparison(asymptotic analysis) between different program implementations. We also compare abstract data types, their operation times, and common Algorithms to tackle complex problems.
  • In class: : We often went over a concept and immediately went on to program a structure or improve lagging code.
  • Learned:
    • Constructing more efficient programs
    • Comparing implementations by time efficiency
    • Learned how to analyze programs at a glance
    • Different structures and their pros/cons
    • Basic algorithms for dynamic programming: divide and conquer, greedy implementations, multi-pointers, tail-recursion, caching etc..
    • Advanced algorithms like: djiskstras, bellmans fords,Kruskal etc..
Grade: 'A'

Systems Programming

  • Summary: Programming in C and advanced programming in Unix environments, including multiprocessing and multithreading
  • In class: In class, we reviewed and implemented basic c programs as well as learned to navigate our local Unix environment using VIM as our text editor. Further along, we implemented more complex programs such as flipping images by altering known pixels, as well as constructing our command line parser. There were also programs related to creating new processes and constructing multi-threaded applications.
    • Learned:
    • Programming in c, including pointers, structs etc..
    • Mastered dynamic memory management
    • Used processes and threads
    • Learned standard input/output
    • Programming in command line interfaces outside of IDEs, including makefiles,gcc, commandline programs and gdb
Grade: 'A'

Tutoring Experience and skills learned

Communication

Communication of central ideas was the most important aspect of my job. I had to adjust my communication style for every individual. Many were reserved and required a more nuanced and non-stimulative approach to conversation. Others loved to chat and were most engaged with high levels of energy and friendly rapport building mixed into our sessions. Regardless, I had to learn to approach each individual in a way that was conducive to their learning style.

Flexibility and learning

Additionally, the most challenging part of my job is having to relearn/rethink central ideas. Often, the way I learned was far from the way others would learn. This required me to relearn or rewire my brain during a session to re-express an idea. Examples of this often came with abstract structures or operating Systems concepts like threading and processes. If the examples I made didn't work, I would just need to rethink an example, or even find a different approach on the fly. Many times, I would often spend time outside of sessions to learn different ways of understanding upcoming topics.

Responsibility

My time tutoring has also instilled a sense of responsibility and proactivity in me. This was because I was not micro-managed and was trusted to run myself, my tutees, and my job responsibilities/sessions on my own. This included me:

  • Creating my own schedule/hours
  • Reporting/creating my own time sheets
  • Seeking out and completing supplemental training on my own
  • Abiding by the institutions rules and regulations
  • Conducting myslef appropriately to the standards of the instituiton and its handbook
  • Addressing my own problems and reach out to the proper channels when necessary.
  • Participating in weekly meetings
This has required me to be diligent in maintaining my work/life balance, keeping myself accountable, and keeping myself disciplined. Ultimately, I find it is safe to say working for FIU has made me a more efficient and responsible person.
