Communication and Cooperation

...

Communication of central ideas was the most important aspect of my job. I had to adjust my communication style for every individual. Many were reserved and required a more nuanced and non-stimulative approach to conversation. Others loved to chat and were most engaged with high levels of energy and friendly rapport building mixed into our tutoring sessions. Regardless, I had to learn to approach each individual in a way that was conducive to learning. I also had to work with individuals with learning disabilities and had to learn how to reliably get information to stick. Cooperation was also necessary as I needed to conduct sessions based on individuals' needs and how they learn best. Many required high-level images and step-by-step illustrations, others required mnemonic devices or visual cues to remember or digest information. I had to learn about every individual and cater to their needs!

Flexibility in learning

Additionally, the most challenging part of my job is having to relearn/rethink central ideas. Often, the way I learned was far from the way others would learn, which required that I would need to relearn or rewire my brain during a session to re-express an idea. Examples of this often came with abstract structures or operating Systems concepts like threading and processes. Often people didn't like the textbook definition of threading: Threading is a lightweight process that executes code sequence and all the data supporting structures such as opened resources, memory map, stack, etc.., while reading this makes practical sense to me( I like to read the documentation and formal definitions as they often have information that makes concepts click in my head), often I found this did NOT make any real sense to most people. Often I found making references to the real world to work the best. This would require I think of formal definitions in a practical or simplified way. One such idea for threads is having a restaurant with one worker ( single thread) handling one job, if the thread is busy computing something ( like cutting vegetables or something else) you have no choice but to wait for the worker to finish to move to the next task. Of course, as a restaurant owner, you would find this inefficient and decide you should hire some workers. so, instead of waiting, you now have multiple workers ( multiple threads ) that can carry out other tasks ( cooking, washing dishes, etc..). While a relatively rough description, the core idea, and rationale become quite apparent to most. If the example didn't work, I would just need to rethink an example, or even find a different approach! It was hard, but one of the most rewarding aspects of tutoring is when it finally clicks for someone.

Responsibility

My time tutoring has also instilled a sense of responsibility and proactivity in me. This was because I was not micro-managed and was trusted to run myself, my tutees, and my job responsibilities/sessions on my own. This included me:
  • Creating my own schedule/hours
  • Reporting/creating my own time sheets
  • Seeking out and completing supplemental training on my own
  • Abiding by the institutions rules and regulations
  • Conducting myslef appropriately to the standards of the instituiton and its handbook
  • Addressing my own problems and reach out to the proper channels when necessary.
  • Participating in weekly meetings
This has required me to be diligent in maintaining my work/life balance, keeping myself accountable, and keeping myself disciplined. Ultimately, I find it is safe to say working for FIU has made me a more efficient and responsible person.
